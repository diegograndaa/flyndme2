A lot of Milan people ended up in London for work, usually something in finance or fashion, and plenty more got scattered after Erasmus or uni and never quite landed in the same place. So when a reunion comes up, half the group is flying out of northern Italy and the other half out of London. Same old problem: everyone checks their own fare, nobody checks what the group pays together, and someone quietly ends up out of pocket.

Here's the bit people miss: Milan doesn't have one airport, it has three, and London is much the same. A cheap fare out of Bergamo on a budget airline has nothing to do with what the same trip costs from Heathrow, and Linate isn't Stansted either. That's why it matters to compare against each person's actual airport, not some vague idea of 'Milan' or 'London', because it genuinely shifts which destination comes out ahead.

This is usually a weekend or bank-holiday thing, two nights and back, so the margins are tight and one daft layover eats half a day. FlyndMe takes everyone's departure city at once and looks for the destination where the group, added up, pays the least, not where one person grabs a steal and the rest get clobbered. It also lays each departure's share on the table, so the jump between the priciest and cheapest seat stays sane.

Frequently asked questions

Why does it matter which Milan airport you compare?

Because Milan splits its flights across Malpensa, Linate and Bergamo, and prices swing a lot depending on which one you use. FlyndMe compares against each person's real airport rather than the city in the abstract, so if you're flying from Bergamo and a mate's coming from Heathrow, you each see your own honest number instead of an average that helps nobody.

Where do the prices come from? Can I trust them?

From recent saved searches, so it's a snapshot to get you oriented fast without a thousand tabs. It's mainly for ranking which destination wins, not pinning the figure to the cent: before booking the price is pulled live, since it changes day to day. Treat it as a compass for the destination, not a closed figure.

How do we add everyone in the group?

There's a group link you drop into the chat, and each person adds their own departure city, so you're not chasing people for airport codes. Once everyone's in, FlyndMe works out the destination where the group as a whole pays least and breaks down what each person would put in, so the split is clear and one person doesn't end up covering triple.
