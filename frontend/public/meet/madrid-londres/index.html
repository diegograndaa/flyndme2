Madrid–London is the classic split: half the crew stayed in Spain and the other half moved to London for work. London has a huge spread of cheap flights across Europe, so it's tempting to pick somewhere that's easy for the London side; but that place can be expensive from Madrid.

The point here is to look at both cities together. London plays several airports (Gatwick, Stansted, Luton), which opens up cheap destinations that don't show from Madrid; the tool compares against the real ticket airport so the price isn't a fiction. Sort by fairness and you can see whether the London-convenient city leaves the Madrid side stranded, or whether there's a middle ground that works for both.

Mind the pound and the calendars: Spanish bank holidays and UK school breaks don't line up, and that moves prices around. The table below comes from cached searches and is indicative. To actually book, check the live price from the tool.

Frequently asked questions

Doesn't London always have cheaper flights?

It usually has more options, but that doesn't mean the city that's cheap from London is cheap from Madrid. That's why the total group cost is compared, not one city.

Which London airport is used?

It's compared against the real ticket airport (Gatwick, Stansted, Luton, Heathrow…), not a generic city code, so the price is the real one.

Can I see it in pounds?

The tool shows euros by default and lets you switch currency for a rough idea; the actual bookable price is on the flight site.
