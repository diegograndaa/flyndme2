No sign-up. Drop in the cities and the destination comes out.

If half your crew lives in Amsterdam and the other half has moved to Lisbon, you know the drill: remote work pulled people south, and the group keeps drifting apart on the map. Schiphol is one of the best-connected airports in Europe and Lisbon flies just about everywhere too, but they sit at opposite ends of the continent. Meeting halfway in distance doesn't mean meeting halfway in price, and that's where it always gets messy.

The trap is assuming the meeting point has to land somewhere geographically between the two. A city smack in the middle can be pricey out of Schiphol or out of Lisbon, while somewhere that looks way off can come out cheap from both, depending on how the routes fall. Eyeballing a map just doesn't cut it: the cheap route from Schiphol and the cheap one from Lisbon almost never point at the same place, and the winner is rarely the obvious dot.

What FlyndMe does is pool both departures and weigh the group's spend as a whole, not one person's lucky fare, with the per-city breakdown in front of you so nobody ends up quietly covering everyone else. The numbers come from searches already stored and refresh live before you book. There's a group link so each person drops in their own city.

Frequently asked questions

Do I need to sign up or pay anything?

No. Drop in everyone's departure cities, hit search, and see the cheapest place for the whole group. No account, no card. When someone's ready to book, they head off to the airline or agency at the end.

Where do the prices come from? Can I trust them?

They come from a back catalogue of searches, so they're built for comparing fast and deciding without opening twenty tabs. They aren't a locked booking, though: the moment someone goes to buy, the price gets pulled again, since it shifts constantly.

We're spread out, how does everyone add their city?

There's a group link you paste into the chat. Each person opens it and adds their departure city and how many are flying from there. Once the list is in, anyone can run the search and get the destination that works best for the whole group.
